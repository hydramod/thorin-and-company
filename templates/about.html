The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the King under the Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin II Oakenshield

Thorin II was born in TA 2746 to Dwarf prince Thráin II in the city of the Lonely Mountain. Early in his youth, he and the other Dwarves of the Lonely Mountain were forced to flee by the dragon Smaug in TA 2770. While in exile, he quickly grew to become a capable warrior. This was demonstrated when he participated in the Battle of Azanulbizar in TA 2799. He fought with one of the Dwarven armies beneath the East-gate of Moria. At some point in the fighting, his shield broke, and using an oaken tree branch found on the ground as a shield, he gained the epithet "Oakenshield," which would remain with him even in death.

Thorin became King-in-Exile of Durin's Folk in TA 2850 after his father went missing, and died in the dungeons of Dol Guldur.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli were nephews of Thorin, who was the elder brother of their mother, Dís. They were also nephews of Frerin, grandsons of Thráin II, and great-grandsons of Thrór. Fíli's most distinguishing feature was his long nose, the longest of any of the Dwarves in Thorin's company.
As the youngest Dwarves, Fíli and Kíli had the sharpest eyes, and were often sent scouting or searching. It was they that found the Front Porch while in the Misty Mountains.
With the other Dwarves, Fíli and Kíli encountered spiders and were later imprisoned by the Elvenking Thranduil. They escaped the cells thanks to Bilbo, traveling down the river in barrels until they reached Lake-town. The brothers accompanied Thorin and Bilbo to Lake-town while the others rested by the river.
At the feet of the Lonely Mountain, Fíli and Kíli scouted Ravenhill and found the Front Gate with Balin and Bilbo. Also, with Bilbo, they found the side-door leading into the mountain.
Both brothers were killed while defending the mortally wounded Thorin Oakenshield in the Battle of Five Armies, and all three were buried with honor.

Balin

Balin was one of the dwarves in Thorin's company. He was a trusted companion and Thorin's second-in-command. Balin played a key role in the quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain. He was known for his wisdom and knowledge, and he often provided guidance and counsel to Thorin. Balin's most notable achievement was his attempt to reclaim the ancient Dwarven kingdom of Moria. Unfortunately, his expedition ended in failure, and he perished there. Despite his fate, Balin's memory lived on as a respected member of Thorin's company.

Dwalin

Dwalin was one of the dwarves in Thorin's company. He was a skilled warrior and Thorin's brother. Dwalin was known for his strong and fearless nature. He fought bravely alongside his companions in the quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain. Dwalin was often seen wielding a large war hammer in battle, showing his proficiency in close combat. Despite the challenges they faced, Dwalin remained steadfast and loyal to Thorin and the quest until the end.

Dori

Known for his strength and endurance, Dori served as the protector and caretaker of his fellow dwarves. With a selfless and nurturing nature, he was often seen lending a helping hand to those in need. Dori's loyalty and unwavering support were a source of comfort to the company during their perilous journey. His steady presence and keen sense of responsibility exemplified the noble qualities that define a true companion.

Ori

Ori, a dwarf with a talent for writing and chronicling their journey, played a crucial role in Thorin's company. With his quill and parchment ever ready, Ori diligently recorded the trials and triumphs of their quest. Known for his gentle and kind nature, he provided a calming presence amidst the challenges they faced. Ori's skills extended beyond writing, as he also displayed bravery in times of danger. His loyalty and unwavering spirit uplifted the spirits of his companions, reminding them of the importance of their mission. Ori's words and actions contributed to the legacy of Thorin and Company, forever etched in the annals of their shared history.

Óin

Óin, a skilled healer and expert in medicinal arts, played a vital role in Thorin's company. With his vast knowledge of herbs, poultices, and remedies, Óin was the primary source of medical care for the group. Whether it was treating wounds, curing ailments, or providing comfort, his skills were indispensable during their perilous journey. Despite the constant dangers they faced, Óin remained focused and dedicated to ensuring the well-being of his companions. Glóin

Glóin, a seasoned warrior and skilled diplomat, brought his wisdom and experience to Thorin's company. As the father of Gimli, a prominent member of the Fellowship of the Ring, Glóin's dedication to the cause was unwavering. Known for his impressive beard adorned with intricate braids, Glóin was a respected voice of reason within the group. He possessed exceptional negotiation skills, often representing the dwarves' best interests during interactions with other races. Glóin's loyalty to Thorin and the desire to reclaim their homeland burned fiercely in his heart, driving him forward amidst countless challenges.

Bifur

Known for the ancient Dwarvish axe embedded in his head, Bifur's story was one of resilience and determination. Despite his injury, Bifur remained an active participant in their quest. Bifur's strength and unwavering spirit were an inspiration to his comrades, showcasing the indomitable nature of the dwarves. Bombur
